# CLAC Dashboard — Complete Guide
**Cleveland Lithuanian American Community**
**clac-website.pages.dev/dashboard**

---

## Getting Started

### Logging In
1. Go to `clac-website.pages.dev/dashboard`
2. Enter your username and password
3. Session lasts 8 hours — you'll be prompted to log in again after that

### Changing Your Password
Go to **Admin → Users** → find your username → click Edit → enter a new password → Save.

> ⚠ Never share your password. Each board member should have their own login.

### The Sidebar
The left sidebar is organized into collapsible groups. Click any group header to expand or collapse it. The dashboard remembers which groups you had open. On mobile, tap the ☰ button in the top left to open the sidebar.

**Groups:**
- **Content** — Events, Announcements, Page Content
- **Site** — Gallery, Media Library, Board Members, Quick Links
- **Community** — Inquiries, Donations, Sponsors
- **Social** — Post Generator, Content Calendar
- **Board** — Board Portal, Activity Log
- **Admin** — Users, Settings

## Content

### Events
The most important section. Everything on the public calendar lives here.

**Creating a New Event:**
1. Click **+ New Event** in the top right
2. Fill in **Tab 1 — Event Details:**
   - Title (English and Lithuanian if available)
   - Date, Start Time, End Time
   - Location and Street Address
   - Category (Cultural, Religious, Festival, etc.)
   - Cover Photo — drag and drop or click to upload (compressed automatically)
   - Description — what the event is, who should come, what to expect
   - RSVP Link — paste your Zeffy ticket link here for paid events
   - Published — check this to make the event visible on the public site
   - Featured — check this to show the event prominently on the homepage
   - Schedule Publish — set a future date to auto-publish (leave blank to publish immediately)

3. After the event happens, come back and fill in **Tab 2 — Story & Photos:**
   - Write the event story/recap (3-5 paragraphs about what happened)
   - Use the **AI Story Writer** — type a few bullet points and hit "Write Story →" to get a full draft
   - Upload event photos (compressed automatically)

4. Then go to **Tab 3 — Share:**
   - Click "Generate Social Posts →" to get ready-to-post Facebook and Instagram captions
   - Copy the Facebook caption → open Facebook → paste and post
   - Copy the Instagram caption + download the image card → open Instagram → upload

**Managing Existing Events:**
- Use the **All / Upcoming / Past** filter buttons to find events
- Click **Edit** on any event to update it
- Check the checkbox next to multiple events → **Delete Selected** to bulk delete
- Draft events (Published unchecked) are invisible to the public

**Scheduled Publishing:**
Set a "Schedule Publish" date on any event and leave Published unchecked. The system runs every morning at 8am ET and automatically publishes anything whose date has passed.

### Announcements
Short news items that appear on the homepage and news section.

**Creating an Announcement:**
1. Click **+ Add New**
2. Fill in Type, Date, Headline, and Body Text
3. Check Published to make it visible
4. Click Save

**Types available:** Community Update, National Organization, Heritage, Membership, Religious, Civic

---

### Page Content
Edit the text on the homepage without touching code.

**What you can edit:**
- Hero headline and subtext (English and Lithuanian)
- The three "About" columns (Who We Are / What We Do / Get Involved)
- Stats strip (70+ Years Active, E. 185th Heritage Corridor)
- Ticker / scrolling banner text

**How to use:**
1. Edit the fields
2. Click **Save Page Content →**
3. Refresh the public homepage to see the changes

## Site

### Gallery
Manage the public photo gallery at `clacleveland.org/gallery`.

**Approving Community Submissions:**
When someone submits photos through the "Were You There?" form on the gallery page, they appear here under **Pending Submissions**. Review the photos, then:
- Click **✓ Approve** to add them to the public gallery
- Click **✕ Reject** to discard them

**Adding Photos to an Event:**
1. Select the event from the dropdown
2. Click **Upload Photos** → select photos from your computer
3. Photos are compressed automatically and added to that event's gallery page

**All Gallery Photos:**
View and delete any photo in the gallery. Hover over a photo to see the delete button.

---

### Media Library
A general photo storage area. Use this to store logos, background images, or any photos you want to use across the site.

- Drag and drop photos to upload
- Click any photo to copy its URL
- Use that URL anywhere — event cover photos, board member photos, etc.

---

### Board Members
Manage the board member grid on the About page.

**Adding a Board Member:**
1. Click **+ New Member**
2. Fill in Name, Role (English and Lithuanian), Email (optional), Bio (optional)
3. Upload a headshot photo
4. Set Sort Order (1 = appears first)
5. Check Published → Save

> Keep names and roles professional — this appears publicly on the About page.

---

### Quick Links
Manage the "Quick Links" sidebar on the homepage.

Add links to frequently referenced resources — National LAC, St. Casimir Parish, community partners. Set the Sort Order to control the display sequence.

## Community

### Inquiries
Everyone who fills out the **Join & Get Involved** form on the public site appears here.

**Managing Inquiries:**
- **New** — just submitted, not yet contacted
- **Contacted** — you've reached out to them
- **Closed** — resolved or no longer active

Click the **Email ↗** button to open a pre-addressed email to that person. Change the status dropdown to track where you are with each person.

> A red badge on the Inquiries menu item means there are new ones waiting.

---

### Donations
Track donations received through the website.

**Fundraising Goals:**
Add specific fundraising goals (One World Day booth, Saturday School supplies, etc.) with a target amount. Update the "Raised So Far" field manually as donations come in, or automatically if Stripe is connected.

**Donation History:**
Shows all donations with donor name, amount, fund, date, and status (pending/completed).

**Connecting Payment Providers:**
Go to **Admin → Settings → Payment & Donations** to add:
- **Zeffy** — free for nonprofits, handles tickets and donations
- **PayPal Giving Fund** — zero fees for registered 501(c)(3)s
- **Stripe** — embedded checkout on the donate page

---

### Sponsors
Manage business and organization sponsors displayed on the Donate page.

**Adding a Sponsor:**
1. Click **+ Add Sponsor**
2. Fill in company name, tier, website, contact info
3. Upload their logo
4. Set start and end dates for the sponsorship year
5. Check Active → Save

**Tiers:**
- **Platinum** ($1,000/yr) — logo in homepage hero, all event pages, social posts, table at annual dinner
- **Heritage** ($500/yr) — logo on homepage and footer, mentioned in event social posts
- **Community** ($250/yr) — logo in footer, thank you social post

## Social

### Post Generator
Generate Facebook and Instagram posts for any event using AI.

**How to use:**
1. Select an event from the dropdown
2. Choose platform (Facebook, Instagram, or Both)
3. Add an optional hint (e.g. "emphasize family-friendly, mention potluck")
4. Click **✦ Generate with AI →**
5. Review the generated posts — edit the text if needed
6. Copy the Facebook post → paste into Facebook
7. Copy the Instagram caption + download the image card → upload to Instagram

> The AI reads the event details and writes platform-appropriate posts. Facebook posts are longer and conversational. Instagram posts are punchy with hashtags.

**Post History:**
Saved drafts appear below the generator so you can reference previous posts.

---

### Content Calendar
Schedule a week of automated social media content from the Lithuanian content library.

**How to use:**
1. Choose a platform (Facebook, Instagram, or Both)
2. Click **✦ Generate Week with AI**
3. The system generates 7 posts pulling from the content library (history, recipes, language, culture, trivia, sports, traditions)
4. Review each post — change status to **✓ Approved** for posts you want auto-posted
5. Skip anything that doesn't feel right
6. Approved posts auto-post at 8am ET on their scheduled date (requires Facebook credentials in Cloudflare)

**Content Library:**
The library has 30+ seeded entries about Lithuanian Cleveland history, cultural traditions, recipes, language words, sports, and trivia. Add your own entries to customize the content mix.

**Categories rotate:**
History → Recipe → Culture → Trivia → Language → Sports → Tradition → repeat

## Admin

### Users
Manage who can log into the dashboard.

**Adding a New User:**
1. Click **+ Add User**
2. Set username, display name, password, and role
3. Click Save

**Roles:**
- **Admin** — full access to everything
- **Editor** — can manage content but not users or settings

**Changing a Password:**
Click Edit on any user → enter new password → Save. Passwords are stored as secure hashes — no one can see the actual password, not even admins.

> After setting up a new user, tell them their username and password directly — do not send via email.

---

### Settings
Site-wide configuration.

**Tabs:**
- **Site Identity** — org name, contact email, Facebook and Instagram URLs
- **Homepage Content** — hero text, about columns, stats, ticker (same as Page Content section)
- **Payment & Donations** — Zeffy, PayPal, and Stripe configuration
- **Social Media** — Facebook Page Token and Page ID for auto-posting

**After changing settings** — click **Save Settings →** and refresh the public site to see changes take effect.

## Technical Notes

**Auto-publishing runs daily at 8am ET.** Any event or announcement with a "Schedule Publish" date in the past gets automatically published. Social media posts marked "Approved" in the Content Calendar also auto-post at this time (requires Facebook credentials).

**Photos are compressed automatically.** You don't need to resize photos before uploading. The system handles it — cover photos compress to 1200×800px, gallery photos to 1600×1200px, avatars to 400×400px.

**The dashboard is not mobile-optimized for complex editing** but works for quick checks — viewing stats, approving inquiries, changing event status. Use a computer for writing event descriptions and uploading photos.

**Session timeout is 8 hours.** If you're logged out unexpectedly, just log back in — nothing is lost.
